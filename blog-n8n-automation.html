Automating a 6-Hour Manual Process with n8n

February 20266 min readOlaitan Hammed

A client came to me with a problem that's more common than most businesses realize: their sales team was spending 6 hours every week on manual work that should have been automated years ago. CRM updates, lead scoring, follow-up emails — all done by hand, copy-pasting between tabs, hoping nothing fell through the cracks.

I built a single n8n workflow that replaced the entire process. Here's how.

The Problem

The client's CRM (HubSpot) was the system of record, but it wasn't connected to anything else. New leads came in from Typeform, LinkedIn messages, email inquiries, and Calendly. Each required manual entry into HubSpot, along with tagging, scoring, and triggering the right follow-up.

Before — Manual Process

  • Check Typeform for new submissions
  • Copy lead data into HubSpot manually
  • Check Calendly for new bookings
  • Cross-reference with existing contacts
  • Score each lead (1–10) based on criteria
  • Send follow-up email from a template
  • Update Slack with daily summary
  • Log everything in Google Sheets

After — Automated Pipeline

  • Webhook captures all sources instantly
  • Auto-creates/updates HubSpot contact
  • Deduplicates against existing database
  • AI scores lead based on 8 criteria
  • Triggers personalized email sequence
  • Posts real-time alerts to Slack
  • Backs up everything to Sheets
  • Daily digest summary at 9 AM

The Architecture

Rather than building four separate workflows for four lead sources, I designed a unified pipeline with a normalizer at the front. Every lead gets transformed into a standard format before hitting the core processing logic.

  1. Ingest — Webhooks capture data from Typeform, Calendly, LinkedIn, and email
  2. Normalize — Transform all sources into a unified lead schema
  3. Deduplicate — Check HubSpot for existing contacts by email
  4. Enrich — Pull company info and social profiles via Clearbit
  5. Score — AI-powered lead scoring based on 8 weighted criteria
  6. Route — High-score leads get immediate Slack alert; others enter email sequence
  7. Act — Create/update HubSpot contact, trigger email, log to Sheets

Key Engineering Decisions

The final workflow had 23 nodes plus an error-handling sub-workflow with 8 nodes:

  • Error handling — If HubSpot's API is down, leads queue in a temp Sheet until retry
  • Rate limiting — Built-in delays to respect API limits on HubSpot and Clearbit
  • Idempotency — Running the same lead twice doesn't create duplicate contacts
  • Monitoring — Failed executions trigger a Slack alert to #ops

The Results

"We went from 6 hours per week of manual work to zero. Leads hit HubSpot faster than we can refresh the page."

  • 6 hours/week → 0 of manual CRM work
  • 12 seconds average lead processing time
  • Zero duplicates in the first 30 days
  • 94% faster lead response time (4 hours → 15 minutes)

Takeaway

The most impactful automations eliminate boring, repetitive, error-prone work that happens every day. If you find yourself saying "we've always done it this way," that's where automation delivers the highest ROI.
